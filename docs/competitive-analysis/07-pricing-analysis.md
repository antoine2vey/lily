## Pricing Patterns & Insights

### Common Price Anchors
- **$29.99/year** is the dominant annual price point (Greg, PictureThis, PlantIn, Lily)
- **$35.99/year** is the premium anchor (Planta — market leader)
- **$4.99–$7.99/month** is the standard monthly range
- **Weekly pricing** ($4.99/week = $260/year) is used by aggressive monetizers (Plantum) and widely criticized by users

### Psychological Pricing Observations
1. **$29.99/year = "less than a coffee per week"** — the most effective framing in the category
2. **Monthly-to-annual discount of 50%+** drives annual conversion (Lily already does this well: $4.99/mo vs $29.99/yr = ~50% savings)
3. **Lifetime options** ($49.99 from Greg) appeal to commitment-averse users but cap LTV
4. **"No credit card for trial"** is a strong trust signal — Lily already uses this

### What Users Hate
- **Weekly subscriptions** (Plantum's $4.99/week) — seen as predatory
- **Paywall before any value** — Blossom's 3-day trial frustrates users
- **Auto-renewal surprises** — top complaint across all apps
- **Core features behind paywall** — Greg locks reminders, Planta locks identification

## Lily's Current Pricing Assessment

### Strengths
- **$4.99/mo and $29.99/yr** — competitively priced, below Planta
- **50% annual savings** — clear value prop for annual conversion
- **7-day free trial with no credit card** — best-in-class trust signal
- **Read-only access after trial** — doesn't lock users out entirely (smart retention)

### Weaknesses
- **5-plant free tier limit** — Planta offers unlimited plants for free; this is the biggest gap
- **No quarterly option** — Planta's $17.99/3mo captures undecided users
- **No lifetime option** — misses the segment that wants to pay once
- **No family/household plan** — missed opportunity with delegation feature

---

## Recommended Pricing Strategy

### Optimal Model: **Generous Freemium + Tiered Premium**

#### Free Tier (Expand)
- **Increase from 5 to 15 plants** — matches the "serious beginner" threshold and undercuts Planta's "unlimited but feature-limited" model
- Keep AI limits (10 chats, 5 scans, 3 IDs) — these are the premium upsell triggers
- Keep all social features free — drives network growth
- Keep achievements free — drives engagement and retention

#### Premium Tier ($4.99/mo — Keep Current)
- Unlimited plants, AI chats, scans, IDs
- Advanced features (diagnosis, delegation, weather integration)
- Priority AI responses

#### Annual ($29.99/yr — Keep Current)
- Same as premium, 50% savings
- Position as "best value" — already well-priced against market

#### New: Quarterly ($12.99/3mo)
- Bridges the gap between monthly and annual
- ~$4.33/mo — slight discount from monthly, encourages longer commitment
- Captures "let me try for a season" users

#### New: Lifetime ($79.99 one-time)
- Higher than Greg ($49.99) but justified by AI features
- Caps LTV but captures commitment-averse segment
- Can limit to promotional periods to create urgency

#### Future: Family Plan ($6.99/mo or $39.99/yr for up to 3 users)
- Natural extension of delegation feature
- Unique in market — no competitor offers this
- Households sharing plant care is a real use case

### Key Pricing Principles
1. **Never paywall basic care reminders** — this is the #1 complaint about Greg
2. **Keep social features free** — network effects drive growth
3. **AI features are the premium moat** — this is what justifies the subscription
4. **Transparent pricing, no dark patterns** — counter the industry's predatory reputation
5. **"Best value at $29.99/yr"** remains the anchor — don't undercut yourself
